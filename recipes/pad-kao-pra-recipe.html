Pad Kao Pra Recipe

A quick and easy Thai dish that's full of flavor. Pad Kao Pra literally means "Holy Basil Stir-fry". It's a stir fry of ground meat with holy basil and chilies typically served over rice with a fried egg on top.

Quck note: Holy Thai Basil is difficult to find state side, so you'll have to subsitute for another type of Basil. Due to accessibility for me I often use Thai Basil, which has a completely different flavor profile to Holy Basil. Italian Basil is a better substitute since it's closer in flavor to holy basil.

Ingredients

  • 2 TBSP neutral oil
  • 3 shallots
  • 10 garlic cloves
  • 4 Thai bird chiles
  • 1lb ground pork
  • 1lb 96/4 ground beef
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1.5 TBSP fish sauce
  • 1.5 TBSP light soy sauce
  • 3 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 3 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1/3 cup chick broth or water
  • Holy Basil Leaves
  1. In a wok or large pan add over medium-high heat, add the oil, shallots and garlic, and fry for about 3 minutes. Add the chilies and cook for another minute. Turn the heat to high, and add the ground pork and beef, breaking it up into small pieces and allowing to crisp up.
  2. Add the sugar, fish sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Stir-fry for another minute then deglaze the pan with chick broth or water. Let the liquid burn off then add the basil and stir till wilted. Serve over rice.
