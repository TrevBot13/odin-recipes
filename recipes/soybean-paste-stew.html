Soybean Paste Stew with beef Recipe

Soybean Paste Stew or Doenjang-Jjigae was called the national dish of Korea by chef and author Maangchi. A very flavorful soup that's rich, savory, and spicy (or not spicy if you want).

Note: Like most soups this is a versatile soup, so feel free to add different types of vegetables or make an anchovy stock to use instead of the water. Adjust the spice to your preference. I like to add more gochujang than I have listed below. I also use more beef than is completely necessary just to add more protein to my diet.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces of beef brisket, thinly sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and small diced
  • 1 medium onion, small diced
  • 1 small zuchinni, small diced
  • 1/4 cup Doenjang, soybean paste
  • 1 tsp Gochujang, hot pepper paste
  • 8 ounces of medium-firm tofu
  • 1 jalapeno
  • 1 scallion
  1. Heat 2qt pot or earthware bowl over medium-high heat. Once hot add brisket and garlic. Cook until beef is no longer pink then add 2 cups of water. Cover pot for ten minutes. Crack the lid if the water starts to boil over.
  2. Add the potato, onion, zuchinni, doenjang, and gochujang. Cover pot and reduce heat to medium. Cook another 10 minutes and crack the lid if needed.
  3. Stir the stew to incorporate the doenjang and gochujang. Add the tofu, jalapeno, and scallion. Cook, uncovered, for 3 more minutes or until the tofu has softened and the stew is bubbling. Serve and enjoy!
